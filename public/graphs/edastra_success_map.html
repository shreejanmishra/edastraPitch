What must go right
5 pillars. Each one can kill
the company if it fails.
Not aspirations — hard dependencies. If any one of these is missing at the wrong time, the model breaks.
01
Exam outcome data loop
Month 9 priority. Every other pillar depends on this proof existing.
Track every student who uses Edastra for 6+ months. Compare their board exam scores to a matched control group. Publish the results. Even a 5% improvement in average score is statistically significant and commercially transformative — it turns a subscription into a necessity, not a nice-to-have.
Highest priority
Failure = paywall never justifiable
02
School partnership pipeline
15 schools by Month 18. Each school = 25–500 users at ₹0 CAC.
The partnership model is what makes ₹180 CAC possible. It requires a dedicated partnerships team member from Day 1, a clear school value proposition (curriculum-aligned, exam-outcome tracked, teacher-friendly dashboard), and a referral incentive — schools that refer other schools get 1 month free. Target GEMS, DPS, Kendriya Vidyalaya chains first for multiplier effect.
Critical
Failure = CAC blows out to ₹1,000+
03
Content quality & velocity
300+ videos by Month 12. AI pipeline keeps cost at ₹230/min.
Content is both the product and the moat. A 300-video library takes a competitor 18 months to replicate at any budget. The AI-assisted pipeline (₹230/min vs ₹1,000–3,000 studio rate) is the structural advantage that makes this economical. Guard the pipeline — it is proprietary. Hire a Content Lead by Month 1 who owns quality standards and curriculum alignment.
High priority
Failure = no moat vs competitors
04
Subscription conversion engine
12–15% conversion by Month 15. Every +1% = ₹12 Cr ARR.
The paywall only works if users experience enough value in the free tier to want more. Design the free tier to create deliberate "cliff moments" — a student gets 3 free VR modules, then hits a paywall before the fourth. Free tier should show learning progress milestones that only continue with subscription. Exam countdown feature (30 days to board exams) is a natural urgency trigger for conversion.
High priority
Failure = ad-only model, low margin
05
Capital efficiency
Burn multiple <1.5x by Month 18. ₹3–4 Cr cash at Series A raise.
Every rupee must generate traceable value. Variable costs (content, marketing) flex down if revenue misses. Fixed costs (core team, infra) are protected. Monthly burn review by founders — not quarterly. The golden rule: never let runway drop below 9 months without a fundraise already in process. Series A process begins at Month 12 when you have 500k users and ₹1 Cr MRR — not at Month 16 when you're already hitting triggers.
Operational
Failure = forced raise at bad terms

What makes Edastra hard to copy
4 moats. Each one takes
18+ months to replicate.
A competitor with unlimited money could copy any one of these. Copying all four simultaneously is practically impossible.
Moat 1 — Content IP library
  • 300+ videos by Month 12 · 1,500+ by Year 3
  • AI pipeline produces at ₹230/min vs ₹1k–3k studio rate
  • Competitor needs 18 months + ₹5 Cr minimum to replicate
  • Content is curriculum-mapped to CBSE/ICSE — not generic
  • Moat deepens with every video added — compounding
Time to replicate: 18 months minimum at any budget
Moat 2 — School partnership network
  • Each school signed = 25–500 users at ₹0 CAC permanently
  • School switching cost is high — admin retraining, data migration
  • School NPS > 60 = schools refer other schools (viral B2B)
  • Outcome data is school-specific — can't be replicated elsewhere
  • Multi-year contracts from batch 100+ give revenue visibility
Time to replicate: 12–24 months of relationship building
Moat 3 — Exam outcome dataset
  • First dataset available Month 9 · grows with every board cycle
  • Proprietary — no competitor can claim this data
  • Becomes the paywall justification parents actually trust
  • Enables price increases over time (outcomes proven)
  • Feeds personalisation engine — improving content quality
Time to replicate: 1 full academic year minimum
Moat 4 — VR first-mover (India K-12)
  • VR UI already live · PoC validates before 2028 launch
  • Byju's VR collapsed — white space exists right now
  • Device partnerships (Meta, Android XR) give preferential placement
  • 3D content library is non-trivial to produce even with budget
  • OpenXR standard means Edastra runs on every future headset
Time to replicate: 2–3 years to reach Edastra's 2028 position
The compounding moat: These four moats reinforce each other. Content quality drives school partnerships. School partnerships provide outcome data. Outcome data justifies VR pricing. VR pricing funds more content. By the time a competitor copies one moat, Edastra's other three have grown further. This is why the flywheel matters more than any single feature.
