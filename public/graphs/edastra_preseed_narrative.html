Series A & B vision · one slide in the deck
VR and MEA are the upside.
Not the ask.
How to frame each correctly so they strengthen, not distract from, the pre-seed pitch.
Wrong framing
"We are launching VR in 2028 and expanding to the Middle East and Africa in 2030."
Investor hears: "They're distracted. 2–4 years away. Too much vision, not enough execution."
Right framing — VR
"VR is already in our live product today — we have a VR section built. We're doing 2 proof-of-concept modules with pre-seed capital to validate learning efficacy. Series A funds the full launch in 2028."
Investor hears: De-risked roadmap. Series A is the unlock. Technical work is already done.
Right framing — MEA
"MEA is the Series B story. Our entry wedge is the Indian diaspora in UAE and KSA — families already familiar with Indian K-12 curriculum. Same product, 3.8x higher ARPU. 4-channel distribution: telco bundles, school licensing, device bundling, and direct B2C."
Investor hears: Specific entry wedge. Defined distribution. Not a vague "global expansion."
The one-slide rule
In the pre-seed deck: VR + MEA = one slide, three bullets. (1) VR PoC already live. (2) Series A = full VR build. (3) MEA = Series B via Indian diaspora entry. No revenue projections on that slide. Full model in data room. Keep the deck focused on the 18-month plan.
This earns you the right to talk about vision without losing the room on execution.
