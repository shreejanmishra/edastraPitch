The device risk and the answer: A VC will ask "why would Tier 1 families buy a ₹35,000 headset for schoolwork?" The answer is threefold — (1) Meta Quest 3S is already trending toward ₹20k by 2027, (2) parents in Mumbai/Delhi already spend ₹30–50k/year on tuition — a one-time device is comparable, (3) Edastra's phone-based VR fallback means students can start on any Android phone and upgrade later. The device is not the barrier.
The Jio angle: Jio has been signalling interest in affordable XR hardware for the Indian market. If Jio launches a ₹5,000–10,000 headset in 2028–29, Edastra is positioned as the content layer on India's largest distribution network. Frame this as optionality in the pitch, not a dependency.

VR content cost vs 2D: A 5-min VR module costs ₹15,000–25,000 to produce (AI-assisted Unity pipeline). An equivalent 2D video costs ₹1,870–3,070. VR is 5–8x more expensive per minute. At 100 modules × ₹20,000 avg = ₹20L production cost. Not prohibitive — and the content IP is permanent.
Revenue model check: At ₹900/yr ARPU and 0.3Mn VR users, Year 1 VR revenue = ₹270 Cr. Annual VR operating cost = ₹28–42L. Net VR contribution margin = ~85%. VR is the highest-margin product in the portfolio — even higher than subscription.
How to present VR to investors
De-risked roadmap.
Not a 2028 promise.
The framing that turns VR from a distraction into a conviction point.
Wrong framing
"We are planning to launch VR in 2028 as a premium product for Tier 1 cities."
Investor hears: 2 years away · unproven · distraction · burning pre-seed on a 2028 feature.
Right framing
"VR is already in our live product today — the UI tab is built. Pre-seed funds 2 proof-of-concept modules to validate learning efficacy. Series A funds the full library. 2028 is the commercial launch, not the start of development."
Investor hears: De-risked · proof-first · capital-efficient · Series A has a clear deployment plan.
The upsell story
"A student acquired at ₹180 CAC on the core app generates ₹250 ARPU. When they upgrade to VR, their ARPU becomes ₹900 — with zero re-acquisition cost. VR is a 3.6x revenue multiplier on an already-paid cohort."
This is the number that excites investors — upsell on paid cohorts with no CAC is pure margin expansion.
